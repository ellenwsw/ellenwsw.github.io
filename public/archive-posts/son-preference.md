# Project overview
As a girl who benefited from China's One-Child Policy in the late 20th century, I can still feel the gender inequality that persists in China. Despite the government's message that "a boy and a girl are the same" when the policy was introduced, societal preferences for sons remained deeply entrenched throughout the 35 years of its implementation. In 2016, China abandoned the One-Child Policy due to the challenges of an aging population, transitioning to a Two-Child Policy, which was further relaxed in 2020 to allow families to have three children. This map is based on data from China's 2020 Census, which for the first time reflects the sex ratio at birth (SRB) for second, third, and subsequent children following the implementation of the Two-Child Policy.

## For your Information
The sex ratio at birth is the number of male births per 100 female births. Without human intervention, the sex ratio at birth is slightly male-biased, with an expected ratio of around 105 boys per 100 girls. However, this ratio can range from 103 to 107 boys per 100 girls in most countries.

## Simplified Process of Map Creation
- First, browsing and collecting data on public data portal.
- Second, I used Excel to filter the data and create a subset focusing on the sex ratio at birth.
- Then, I imported the data into ArcGIS Pro to perform the data analysis and create the maps.
- Finally, I exported the three layouts to Adobe Illustrator to refine the appearance of the text.
